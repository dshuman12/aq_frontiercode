# Leek Tart - extended notes

A second pass at Leek Tart after a few more cookings.

## A history

I first made Leek Tart on a quiet Saturday and it became part of the regular
rotation almost immediately. The first few times I followed the recipe to
the letter; over time I made small adjustments based on what was on the
shelf or in the fridge. Most of those tweaks survived; one or two have
been quietly retired.

## Ingredient substitutions that worked

When I was out of the main leek tart component I tried two
substitutions over different weeks. Both were acceptable but neither was
quite as good. I noted the closest replacement here for next time:

- ingredient A: works in a pinch, but loses about 15% of the dish's
  character. Worth keeping the canonical ingredient on hand.
- ingredient B: surprisingly good. Slightly different but no regrets.

## Tools that earn their place

A heavy pan or pot is the only specialty equipment. Everything else can be
done with a knife and a wooden spoon. I tried a fancier setup for a while
and it didn't change the result.

## Storage and reheats

Leftovers keep for ~3 days in a sealed glass container in the fridge. The
texture is best on day one but the flavor is arguably better on day two.
Beyond day three the bright notes drop off; reheat with a splash of
water or a fresh squeeze of lemon to wake the dish up again.

## What I'd add

- A chunk of cheese on top works for some moods and not others.
- A drizzle of really good olive oil at the end is rarely a bad call.

## Pairings I've tried

- A glass of dry white wine: yes.
- Sparkling water with a wedge of citrus: yes.
- Iced coffee: surprisingly fine for a weekend lunch version.

## Failure modes

The two times this dish has been a disappointment were:
1. Way too much salt early. The fix is to add salt in stages and taste
   between additions.
2. Boiling instead of simmering. The texture suffers and the flavor
   becomes muddier. Keep the heat at a lazy bubble.

## When I make it most often

Cool weeknight evenings, ~once every other week. Sometimes more during
the colder months when I'm leaning into anything warm and slow.
